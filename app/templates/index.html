Data Science Club of UWA

Established in 2019, The Data Science Club of UWA is a SOC-affiliated club committed to creating a supportive internal support network among UWA Data Science students, and preparing them for the industry outside of university.

What is Data Science?

Data Science is not about making models, visualisations, nor writing code. These are all important, but ultimately just cogs moving a much bigger wheel. Data Science in a general sense is about solving problems to maximise the impact of data. This means transforming data into solutions such as useful insights, data products and product recommendations.

With the emergence of big data over the last 2 decades, Data Science has found itself in high demand. Companies are collecting mass amounts of data (big data), from which they need to extrapolate meaningful information (data mining). Data mining on such a large scale requires computer science, and this integration - using computer science to perform data mining - is the formula that equates to data science.

What does a Data Scientist do?

Work within the field can be split distinctly into 6 major groups. At the most fundamental levels are data collection, storage and transformation. These work groups can entail tasks such as logging and documenting user data and/or user generated content, creating infrastructures to store this data on, and cleaning and visualising data. Above this is data aggregation. In this group a data scientist may be expected to analyse and draw insights from data, creating metrics to define and determine whether a product is performing successfully, and A/B testing to compare the performance of 2 different products or strategies. At the very highest and most complex tier of work within the field is learning and optimisation. This work group deals artificial intelligence and machine learning, fields set to change the world in an exciting and dramatic way.
