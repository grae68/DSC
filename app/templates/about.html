Who are we?

Data science is more than just a buzzword. It is a rapidly growing multidisciplinary field that offers opportunities and possibilities for advancement over a vast range of industries. In the last few years the University of Western Australia (UWA) has opened its doors to students interested in this exciting field of study by establishing its Data Science program.

The founding members of the Data Science Club of UWA (DSC) team, Sam Hobson, May-Lyn Hu and Cesar Gonzalez, discovered during their studies that because data science was a relatively new addition to UWAâ€™s course offerings there was no unique space for students like themselves to share their passion for this new discipline. A combination of statistics and computer science studies, no other student club existed that focused on data science or represented data science students. With the aim of developing a space for like-minded students to connect with one other and industry professionals through their interest in data and the technological advancements possible through this ever-evolving field, the Data Science Club of UWA was formed in February 2019, before becoming SOC affiliated in March 2019.

Awards and Accolades

Best New Club Award 2019

Each year, the Societies Council (SOC) of the UWA Student Guild provide an award for the best performing newly affilliated SOC club. Establishing a new club on campus is no easy feat, and this award is designed to bring recognition for those who not only find their footing, but display outstanding achievement right out of the gate.

Through the hard work and dedication of May Hu and the newly formed team, 2019 saw this award fall in the hands of the Data Science Club of UWA. Some of the criteria that deemed the club worthy of such an accolade included:

  • Establishing a strong presence amongst the students, faculty and data science industry through various club events, with a strong priority on networking
  • Creating a sense of community amongst members by leading with a welcoming culture from the committee and hosting social events such that students have plenty of opportunity to interact
  • Engagement with other EMS clubs via collaborative endeavours and mutual support
  • Raising awareness of data science amongst the university and community
  • Participation in numerous Guild events such as Club Carnival, as well as engaging the wider university community with talks from industry experts, forums, study nights, a networking night, workshops and social events
  • Engagement of multiple cohorts accross the university, with members from the UWA business school, international students, varying age groups, postgrads and undergrads, a diverse range of ethnicities, and the LGBTQI+ community
  • Demonstration of responsible event management and planning
  • Attendance at SOC meetings and compliance with other Guild standards and procedures
  • Accumulation of noteable sponsors including Visagio full-time, and Quantum Black, Hayes and Rio Tinto for specific events
  • Multiple sold out club events
